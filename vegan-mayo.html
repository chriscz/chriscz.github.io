Vegan Mayo

Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp chick pea water (brine), this replaces the egg of normal mayo
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 - 1/2 tsp salt (or more to taste)
  • 1 - 2 tsp of whole grain mustard
  • 250ml of flavour neutral oil: canola, flax seed or a mixture of these with sunflower (not olive oil)

Equipment

  • stick blender
  • a mixing bowl/container that is only slightly broader than your stick blender, this prevents splattering and ensures that the oil will cover the blender blade.

Instructions

  • Add all ingredients except the oil to a mixing bowl
  • Have someone slowly add the oil while you blend the mixture without stopping.

You will find that that the mayo does not take till a critical point is reached: right after the blade is completely submersed in oil.

  • You can move the blender stick in an upright stirring fashion, with some up and down motion. The up-down motion takes precedence when the mixture begins to thicken.

Fixing a Runny Mess

  • If your mayo stays runny, then you may have: added the oil too quickly, used stale chick pea water, or made a mistake while blending. All is not lost, however!

If a batch fails or comes out runny, you can still save it by preparing another correctly. As the second batch begins to thicken, slowly pour the failed mixture in while thoroughly blending it.

Tips

  • You can make plain mayo mixture without the mustard seed, as far as I could tell only the chick pea water (and maybe 1/2 tbsp of lemon juice) is really necessary to get the mayo to thicken.
  • You can use the chickpeas to make a yummy hummus
