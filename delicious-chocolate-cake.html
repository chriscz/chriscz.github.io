Delicious Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

Dry

  • 3 cups self-raising flour
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 3 pinches of salt
  • 3 80g slabs of chocolate (alternatively melted and mixed with wet batter)

Wet

  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 cup of vegetable oil
  • 500g plain double cream yoghurt ~ 2 cups
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions

  • preheat oven to 180C (~350F)
  • use a food processor or grater to make the chocolate fine. Most of it should be very, but if a few are chunky that's also ok.
  • Alternatively, melt and blend into the wet ingredients
  • Mix all the dry ingredients thoroughly
  • Mix all the wet ingredients thoroughly, separately from the dry ingredients
  • Add wet batter to the dry ingredients, and mix till smooth
  • spray baking tray with non-stick spray or smear with oil / butter
  • pour batter into baking tray
  • bake for 40min, use a fork to test if it's ready.
  • Remove from the oven and cover cake for 5-10 minutes to keep it from drying out (if you're adding sauce it shouldn't be a problem).

Toppings (Optional)

Chocolate Sauce

This sauce is still experimental. There are quite a few ways to prepare it, this is one way. You can change the quantities to suit your taste.

Ingredients

  • 400ml milk
  • 1 cup of hot chocolate
  • 1/4 cup of sugar (optional)

Instructions

  • simmer for around 20-30 minutes, stirring continuously (4-5 on a 6 scale stove top).
  • or 10 minutes on 50% heat in the microwave
  • The sauce won't really thicken, but should taste chocolatey, and not too sweet.
  • When the cake is done baking, make tiny holes in the surface with fork then
  • pour sauce over the cake, making sure it doesn't get soggy. Stop before it does!
  • If any sauce remains, keep it for later when you eat the cake

Caramel treat

As an alternative to the chocolate sauce, spread some caramel treat on the cake after it has cooled down.

The finishing touches

  • let it cool for 20-30 minutes before serving
