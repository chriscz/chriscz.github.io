Poetry
Chris — Sat 28 July 2018

Shorts

Mad mind body
bound by belief
Lost in its triumphs
bound for defeat

Gathering moments
as lid to get paid
Or like chocolates to eat
when the years run dry

The ironic meaning of life
lies between the action of deciding
and the belief in having had a choice.

The soul-deep thirst that eats away
at our lives every day

In the face of conviction of a truth so deep,
a moment so quenching,
how can man be blamed for losing his balance?

A crimson drop of authenticity in a shadowy sea of blind conformance.

In the one hand I held the other small:
The backside of yours as our bodies touched.

Each part of me relaxed, and was at ease
with the jigsaw pieces well aligned.
