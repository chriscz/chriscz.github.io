Poetry
Chris — Thu 10 August 2017

Ariam

Be a child,
a playful one
pull your face,
Look the fun
worry not what others think,
throw those thoughts down at the door,
ask the moment
and it gives more.

Whisper in my ear,
the feelings which you fear:
those moments lost to youth,
those one's you've let slip past.

Tell me dear, truly speak
Let me see you as you are,
drop all facade and paint to floor,
and show me what you hid before.

Show me pictures, and tell me tales,
the sentimental,
the little things:
the defining walls of your heart
What's your foundation?
Where did it start?

Walk with me in silent tones
going deep to leave behind
conventional heart and mind.

Let painting become painter and
you the canvas for its story.
What spectrums do you feel?

Forget responses conditioned,
present me with your moment:
where I dance with you,
dressed in what you are
not in what you think you have to be.
