Poetry
Chris — Thu 11 May 2017

Sunset

The chilly winter breeze,
speaks to me tonight.
The sun's last caress of the mountain's cheek,
Red pink purple -- gone.
Rustling leaves - the last protest.
Moments - these, my happiness.

Speak wind. I will hear your
whispers from the trees.

Far and near: no silence yet, as dogs protest
foreign man or beast
which strayed past his home.

Lay to rest your qualms, your dissatisfaction.
Let be the moment you're in.
Be with it: fears, tears, love and sin.
