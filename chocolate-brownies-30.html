Anyhow, I decided to bake brownies again! This time I've tweaked a bit more at the recipe, and in the future I hope to make a completely vegan and gluten-free brownie. Shout out to Yana for reminding me that some are actually gluten intolerant; not sure if she is though.

As you'll soon see, most of the recipe remains unchanged. These brownies are fudgey when cold, and slightly !moist! when at room temperature. In the fridge they can keep for at least 2 weeks, probably longer.

As cake flour containse less protein than white bread flour, you'll find that these brownies are slightly softer and have a more pleasant texture than the 2.0 iteration.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup cake flour (71g)
  • 1/2 cup white bread flour (71g)
  • (I always baked with pure white bread flour, as it increased chewiness)
  • 1 tsp salt (5g)
  • 2 tbsp cacao powder (15g)
  • 3x80g dark semi-sweet chocolate bars (I used the Nestl√© Albany brand)
  • TIP Replace one of the above bars with a peppermint crisp bar
  • 2 tsp instant coffee (Nescafe classic or Jacobs) (optional)
  • 1 cup of butter (227g, salted is fine)
  • 1 cup of treacle sugar (about 220g - 230g)
  • 5 eggs, at room temperature
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract (10g)

Notes

  • This recipe makes 24 brownies (cut them 6 by 4)
  • You will be mixing the dry ingredients into the bowl of wet ingredients later on in this recipe.
  • Do not use your oven's convection fan
  • Our oven only has a top-element, but it does have top and bottom heat setting, which I used.
  • If your oven heats unevenly you should turn the tray around in the middle of the bake

Utensils

  • A medium sized pot for preparing the chocolate
  • A mixing bowl for the dry mixture
  • Baking tray (9x13 inch 22x33cm) glass is best, but metal is also fine. A metal pan will bake faster (25 minutes) whereas glass will take around 30 minutes.
  • Kitchen fork or tablespoon for stirring melted chocolate

Oven & Baking Tray prep

You can do these after the whole batter is completed, as there is a resting time before starting baking. 1. Preheat the oven to 180 C and use the central tray. 2. Oil / butter / Spray-'n-Cook your baking tray

Dry mix

  • Mix the following together
  • Cacao powder
  • Salt
  • Flour

Wet mix

You can alternatively use a pot on low heat to melt the butter, chocolate and sugar instead of

  1. Break chocolate bars into single blocks, while inside their packet if possible.
  2. Cut butter into smaller pieces and place in your pot, then turn it up to a low heat. The butter MUST NOT sizzle, it must merely melt. Stir occasionally.
  3. Add the Chocolate bars and coffee to your pot, stirring to melt and to prevent it from burning to the bottom. If your stove is really hot then occasionally remove the pot from the stove.
  4. Stir the treacle sugar into the chocolate sauce until mostly dissolved. You might find that the batter feels sandy from the sugar. This is normal.
  5. Remove the pot from the heat and let it cool for at least 20 minutes, stirring the batter every 10 minutes to prevent clumps from forming. NOTE trust me on this, otherwise you'll be eating scrambled egg later.
  6. When cooled, add the vanilla extract.
  7. Whisk in 3 eggs till combined, then add the 2 remaining eggs and whisk until combined.
  8. Slowly add the dry mix to the bowl while folding it into the mixture using a spoon or rubber spatula.
  9. Pour the batter into your baking tray and smooth it out
  10. Let the mix stand for 10 minutes so that any bubbles rise to the top.
  11. Move oven rack to the middle position.
  12. Bake in the oven for 20 minutes, then checking every 1 minute, till a toothpick put into the centre of the pan comes out with only a few moist crumbs sticking to it.

Post Baking

  1. Cover the baking pan with a clean dish cloth and let it stand for 10 minutes.
  2. Loosen the brownies from the base of the tray
  3. On a clean surface, turn the tray over, leaving the brownies covered for a further 10 minutes.
  4. Cut into the appropriate shape (6x4 for the suggested 24 brownies)
