Chris — Thu 26 July 2018

Woven

Lives weaved
at the point of a needle.
So thin the thread,
so tiny the foundation. Making the truth assumed
as fragile as the existence of the tip. Or does it?
Could the weave have been
had the needle not been
by some divine convention?
But surely only a fool would posit
the immediate existence
of a woven rug?
