Not-so-sweet Chocolate Brownies

These brownies are fudgy, and absolutely delicious. I have cut back on the sugar in the original recipe, and it produced a lovely, not-too-sweet brownie. Will definitely be making these again!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups (177 grams) of all-purpose-flour (I used white bread flour, and it turned out great)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp cacao powder
  • 3x80g dark chocolate bars (I used the Nestl√© Albany brand)
  • 1.5 tsp instant coffee (Nescafe classic or Jacobs) (optional)
  • 1 cup (227g) of butter
  • 1 - 1.5 cups of brown sugar
  • 5 eggs, at room temperature
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  • You'll be mixing the dry ingredients into the bowl of wet ingredients later on in this recipe.
  • This recipe makes 24 brownies (cut them 6 by 4)

Utensils

  • Tablespoon or large spoon for stirring melted chocolate
  • Saucepan
  • Baking tray (9x13 inch 22x33cm) glass is best, but metal is also fine
  • One heat-proof bowl for the batter (melting over the saucepan)
  • One bowl for the dry mix

Oven & Baking Tray

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 C
  2. Oil / butter your baking tray

Dry mix

  • Mix the following together
  • Cacao powder
  • Salt
  • Flour

Wet mix

  1. Bring water to a simmer in a saucepan
  2. Add the following to your other bowl
  3. Chocolate bars
  4. Butter, and
  5. Coffee
  6. Stir until smooth, then turn off the stove
  7. Add the sugar to the chocolate mixture
  8. Stir in until dissolved or it doesn't dissolve anymore
  9. Remove the bowl from the pan and let it stand till it's at room temperature
  10. Add vanilla extract
  11. Whisk in 3 eggs till combined, then add the 2 remaining eggs and whisk until combined.
  12. Slowly add the dry mix to the bowl while folding it into the mixture using a spoon or rubber spatula.
  13. Pour the batter into your baking tray and smooth it out
  14. Bake in the oven for about 30 minutes, till a toothpick put into the centre of the pan comes out with only a few moist crumbs sticking to it.

Post Balking

  1. Cover the baking pan with a clean dish cloth and let it stand for 15 minutes.
  2. Loosen the brownie from the base of the tray
  3. On a clean surface, turn the tray over, leaving the brownies covered for a further 15 minutes.
  4. Cut into the appropriate shape (6x4 for the suggested 24 brownies)
  5. Keeps for 3 days at room temperature or longer when kept in the refrigerator.
