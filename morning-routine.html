Morning Routine

There has been one thing that has carried me through these last few months, and has prevented a relapse to my morning depression - routine.

I used to wake up every morning and reflect on the many ways that my life has not succeeded and all the ways the I am still inferior. These thoughts are pretty automatic, and there isn't much you can do to stop them from a pure will perspective. So I tried some apps, the first was one that I had used a few years prior which helped me start a morning routine: Fabulous, developed by a team at Duke University under the lead of behavioural economist Dan Ariely which you might remember from this famous Ted talk on the Economist subscription model.

Back then it was useful, right now I didn't even want to get out of bed. I HAD to make a plan. I set out to use my alarm app Sleep as Android, and looked at what it had to offer in terms of getting up.

I had used the captcha solving before, but now needed a stronger dose of force: The QR Scanner. This scanner supports normal barcodes, so I set it up to a coffee tin in my kitchen. Caption: The coffee tin of redemption.

The first week was especially hard, and I knew that I had to chain several simple habits together, so I added the following:

  1. drink a glass of lemon water while in the kitchen,
  2. go back to your room and do some push-ups -- 12 at the least --,
  3. take a shower,
  4. then meditate.

I'll admit; some days I fucked up as I went out the night before, and instead of completing the routine, I would get back in bed and sleep a few more hours.

However, in the last 3-4 weeks this has proven to be the most significant change I've made this year. It has allowed me to do some of the things I would usually have to rush, such as my meditation. It just makes for a better overall day.

I have now adapted my routine to include some morning contemplations: Reading Marcus Aurelius's Meditations, and reflecting on questions that I have typed for myself.
